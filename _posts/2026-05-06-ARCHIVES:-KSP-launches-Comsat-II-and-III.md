###### Author: lunatrifx

The Kerbal Space Program has moved quickly when it comes to accomplishing its goals of establishing the CommNet system and the groundwork of its Deep Space Network.

ComSat II and III launched from Kerbal Space Center on May 06, 2026 at 19:30:24 PST, roughly two days after the launch of ComSat I. These two microsatellites carry experimental items on board, as KSP plans to perform a trial run for future satellites.

ComSat II carries a barometer, and in low Kerbin orbit, this allows scientist to better understand where the planet's atmosphere ends and where space begins. This also allows scientists and engineers at KSP to have a good starting point when it comes to physiological data for its astronauts. 

ComSat III is equipped with a magnetometer, allowing for experiments to commence pertaining to Kerbin's magnetic field strength and direction. With a magnetometer, it is sent out to complete an eliptical orbit; Comsat III will travel roughly 1/4 of the distance to the Mun at 2,750 kilometers at its furthest, and come its closest to Kerbin at 80 kilometers.
